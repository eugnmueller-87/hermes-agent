# Content Methodology

## Content Pillars
1. **Building in public** — showing what I'm building, why, what broke, what I learned
2. **Procurement + AI** — practical intersection of my professional background and new skills
3. **Autonomous agents** — architecture, design decisions, tradeoffs
4. **Career transition** — procurement professional → AI builder, the honest version

## Post Types
| Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Build update | Weekly | Show progress on Hermes/Icarus |
| Lesson learned | Biweekly | One concrete insight from building |
| Market signal | As relevant | Interesting finds from Hermes data |
| Career reflection | Monthly | The transition story |

## What Makes a Post Worth Writing
- I learned something non-obvious
- A Hermes signal turned out to be significant
- I made a decision that surprised me in retrospect
- Something broke in an interesting way

## What Does NOT Make a Post
- "AI is changing everything" (no personal story)
- Rephrasing someone else's article
- Tool announcements without context on why I use them
- Anything that starts with "Excited to share"

## Signal-to-Post Workflow
1. Hermes flags a HIGH urgency item with `is_significant = true`
2. Review the item: is there a procurement angle?
3. Draft a post using the brand voice template
4. Connect to personal experience or a specific decision
5. Post via Icarus LinkedIn skill after review
